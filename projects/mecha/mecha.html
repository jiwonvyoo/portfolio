Context, Interface, and Users Impacted

In fast-paced clinical settings, every second counts. Our team partnered with Mecha Health, a startup building AI-powered tools to accelerate radiology workflows. Their mission: reduce the time it takes to analyze an X-ray from one scan per hour to just five minutes. We were tasked with designing an intuitive desktop interface for radiologists and clinical techs—helping them move quickly, confidently, and with minimal cognitive overhead. Though patients never see this tool directly, they benefit from the efficiency it brings to diagnosis and care delivery.

Sketches

We began with rapid low-fidelity sketches to explore key design principles: clarity, speed, and trust. The sketches helped us test layout hypotheses and prioritize what matters most—like X-ray visibility and streamlined interaction.

Desktop Sketch 1

  • We centered the X-ray display to align with radiologists' visual habits, while segmenting AI analysis in a side panel for quick review.
  • A bottom carousel allowed seamless navigation across scans without breaking focus.

Tablet Sketch 1

Design Decisions

From sketches to structure, every decision was made with one question in mind: what would support radiologists the most during high-stakes diagnosis?

Homepage

  • We kept the landing page minimal—anchoring it in trust. A clean intro, clear mission, and fast path to action.

Folder

  • We designed a centralized scan hub that emphasized recall and organization. Drag-and-drop uploads, timestamped scan cards, and preview thumbnails gave users a clear mental model.
  • We also considered future enhancements, like review status indicators, to avoid redundant reads and boost review efficiency.

Upload

  • Upload flow was intentionally spare: one large CTA with a progress bar, supporting quick, confident actions under time pressure.

Diagnosis

  • This screen became our focal point. A large X-ray window provided clarity, with dynamic annotations linked to AI-flagged findings.
  • We enabled toggling symptoms on/off and offered a clean sidebar with diagnosis suggestions and approval/edit actions. The bottom carousel supported lateral navigation across scans.

Critique Highlights

  • Need for login/profile system
  • Better visual markers for scan approval
  • Increased interactivity and feedback cues
  • Clarified edit/approve functionality
  • Improved carousel interaction and scan orientation

Post-Critique Changes

  • Profile Icon: We implemented persistent navigation and login for continuity.
  • Carousel: Added directional arrows and converted layout to vertical—easier thumb reach and visual clarity.
  • Visual Language: Replaced dense text with color-coded icons and collapsible tabs to reduce overload.
  • Edit/Approve Toggle: Removed unnecessary options to streamline user path.

Visual Polish

We unified the product with a muted, clinical color palette (navy, white, gray) and subtle chrome-tab design cues, reinforcing the product’s professional tone without feeling sterile.

User Testing

We observed how users interacted with the live prototype, asking them to locate the spot analysis and identify an AI-generated summary. This tested their ability to navigate, interpret, and act within the interface under minimal instruction.

Findings

  • Most users followed the same navigation path—indicating strong visual hierarchy.
  • However, users clicked scan nodes before opening the summary, unintentionally skipping the app’s main feature.

This insight was critical. It told us that even well-designed features need stronger visibility cues or default states. For future iterations, we’d surface the summary by default—so users immediately engage with the AI-powered diagnosis that defines Mecha’s value.

Reflection

This was one of the most collaborative, research-grounded design processes I’ve experienced. Working across disciplines and iterating fast taught me how to balance bold ideas with rigorous feedback. Our design evolved with every critique—and that evolution was the most valuable part.

What I’m most proud of is the clarity we built into a high-stakes product. Our design doesn’t just work—it supports decision-making, reduces stress, and ultimately contributes to better patient care.
